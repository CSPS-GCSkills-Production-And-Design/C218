Canada, a Constitutional Monarchy

Long before 1867, Canada was inhabited by distinct Indigenous Peoples, divided into separate, independent nations with institutions of their own, governing themselves by their own laws.

Canada is a constitutional monarchy, a federation, and a democracy. The Constitution Act, 1867, provides that there shall be one Parliament for Canada, consisting of three distinct elements: the Crown, the Senate, and the House of Commons.

The Monarchy and the Crown, represented by the Governor General, is the head of state. The responsibility for lawmaking in Canada is shared among one federal, ten provincial, and three territorial governments.
